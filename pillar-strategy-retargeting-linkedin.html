Pillar GTM Playbook: LinkedIn Retargeting

Validation-layer paid social for warm audience recapture and lead form completion.

Retargeting/Research | LinkedIn Retargeting Ads

Validation Layer

1. Segment Pool Parameters

Populate LinkedIn Matched Audiences through strict user-action signals:

  • Website Performance Segment: Users tracking across pillarplatform.co.uk over a rolling 90-day window, filtered by visits to features or pricing plans.
  • Form Abandonment Segment: High-priority targets who accessed the booking flow interface but closed out before completing verification.
  • Engagement Pools: Newsletter subscribers and individuals who interacted with top-of-funnel Single Image or Document items.

2. Eight Creative Concept Families

Deploy varied messaging angles with native LinkedIn formats (Text Ads, Spotlight, Single Image, Carousel, Video, Lead Gen Forms) targeting warm-intent segments at scale:

Concept 1: The Donor Sheet Graveyard

Format: Single Image + Text Overlay

Headline: Your donor spreadsheet just quit
Body: "Actually, I quit 3 years ago. You just didn't notice."
Overlay Text: SPREADSHEETS KILL GROWTH
CTA: Book a 15-min demo

Angle: Humorous personification of Excel as a unreliable employee. Plays into guilt/pain of manual processes. Expected CTR: 2.8-3.5%

Concept 2: The Salary Breakdown (Admin Hours)

Format: Carousel (3 cards)

Card 1: "Your operations manager spends 12+ hours/week on data entry"
Card 2: "That's £18,000+ annually on spreadsheet maintenance"
Card 3: "Pillar automates it. Hire someone who grows your mission instead."
Overlay Text: STOP WASTING PAYROLL

Angle: Quantifies the hidden cost of manual processes. Data-driven, CFO-resonant. Expected CTR: 3.2-4.1%

Concept 3: The Donor Journey Chaos Map

Format: Single Image (flowchart-style)

Visual: Messy arrows bouncing between 5+ tools with X marks and frustrated faces
Headline: Your donors bounce through 5 different systems
Body: Website → Email platform → CRM → Form tool → Payment processor. One unified platform feels like magic.
Overlay Text: CONSOLIDATE TODAY

Angle: Visual complexity overwhelm. Relatable operational frustration. Expected CTR: 3.0-3.8%

Concept 4: The Retention Scorecard

Format: Spotlight Ad + Lead Gen Form

Headline: [First Name], your retention rate is costing you
Body: UK charities lose 40% of donors year-1 due to poor follow-up. Get your retention audit.
Form CTA: I want to improve retention
Overlay Text: ARE YOU LOSING 40%?

Angle: Personalized benchmark anxiety + data magnet. High-intent audience. Expected Form Completion: 18-25%

Concept 5: The Platform Comparison Smackdown

Format: Carousel (4 cards)

Card 1: "Fundraising platform comparison (honest version)"
Card 2: [Competitor A] + [Competitor B] + [Competitor C] stacked with gaps highlighted
Card 3: "Pillar" with all gaps filled + extra benefits
Card 4: "Skip 6 months of shopping around. Talk to us."
Overlay Text: ALL-IN-ONE ADVANTAGE

Angle: Competitive differentiation without naming competitors directly. Decision-stage messaging. Expected CTR: 2.5-3.2%

Concept 6: The Ramadan Readiness Checklist

Format: Single Image + Text (Islamic calendar hook)

Headline: 30 days to Ramadan. Are you ready?
Body: ☐ Donation forms optimised ☐ Email sequences scheduled ☐ Team bandwidth available ☐ Data unified. Stop improvising.
Overlay Text: PREP NOW FOR PEAK SEASON

Angle: Seasonal urgency + faith-relevant calendar. Charity-specific pain point. Expected CTR: 3.5-4.3%

Concept 7: The Software Bloat Roast

Format: Video (15-20 sec, UGC style)

Scene: Admin opens laptop, opens tool #1, then tool #2, then tool #3... pulls up spreadsheet... looks at camera defeated.
Voiceover: "This is your life with 6 separate platforms."
Scene 2: Same admin, now showing Pillar dashboard, smiling, closing all other windows.
Voiceover: "This is your life with Pillar."
Overlay Text: ONE PLATFORM. SANITY RESTORED.

Angle: Relatable comedy. Emotional/aspirational. High engagement potential. Expected Video View Rate: 45-55%

Concept 8: The Trust & Proof Stack

Format: Lead Gen Form (Retargeting Close)

Headline: See how Orphans In Need grew 25% in 18 months
Body: 30 min conversation. No sales pitch. Real metrics. Download the case study.
Overlay Text: PROVEN RESULTS
Form Fields: First Name, Last Name, Work Email, Role, Charity Size

Angle: Social proof trigger + educational resource. Lower-friction ask. Expected Form Completion: 22-30%

4. Offer and Destination Mapping

  • Form starters: send to short booking route with low-friction scheduling copy.
  • Document engagers: route to deeper educational assets before hard CTA.
  • Pricing-page visitors: route to value-comparison and implementation-confidence assets.
  • Competitor-page visitors: route to switching frameworks and migration-risk explainers.

5. Lead Quality Controls

Audience Hygiene

  • Exclude active opportunities and booked demos daily.
  • Suppress recent form submitters from repeated lead-gen asks.
  • Separate customer advocacy audiences from prospecting pools.

Performance Hygiene

  • Review lead quality by ad format, not only CPL.
  • Downgrade high-volume/low-quality audiences quickly.
  • Protect budget for audience tiers with strongest meeting rates.

6. Optimisation Rhythm

  • Twice weekly: check delivery health, recency-window spend balance, and suppression integrity.
  • Weekly: review lead quality by audience and message family and refresh the weakest creative pair.
  • Monthly: re-tier audience windows based on conversion lag and sales feedback patterns.
