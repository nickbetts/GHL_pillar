When Your Data Arrives via FTP

Orphans & Mead's donor data came from their legacy system in one way: a CSV file dropped via FTP every night. It worked in theory. In practice, it meant someone manually pulled reports, waited for the overnight batch, checked for errors, and manually reconciled discrepancies.

During fundraising campaigns, this lag was painful. They'd run a campaign on Tuesday. The data didn't sync until Wednesday night. Reports weren't available until Thursday morning. By then, the campaign momentum was gone and they'd lost the ability to optimise live.

Real-time decisions were impossible. They couldn't see which pages were converting, which donors were recurring, or which campaigns were working until everything was already over. They'd move to the next appeal without ever understanding what happened.

Killing the FTP Drop

Moving to Pillar meant replacing that CSV/FTP workflow with real-time data sync. Donations now appear in their system immediately. Email lists update automatically. Campaign performance is visible as it happens, not days later.

The team didn't have to change how they fundraise. They just stopped waiting for reports. Someone runs a campaign Tuesday, checks performance Wednesday morning with actual data, and can optimise or scale immediately. No batch delays. No manual reconciliation.

What sounds like a small technical detail actually unlocked a fundamentally different way of working. They went from reactive (campaign ends, check results) to responsive (campaign running, optimise in real time).

Campaign Iteration That Actually Happens

Real-time reporting changed which campaigns they run. With the old system, they'd set a campaign up and let it run to completion before checking what worked. Now they start a campaign, watch the data come in, and within 24 hours, they know what's resonating with their donor base. That insight becomes the next campaign.

Donor retention improved because they could finally see which giving levels led to ongoing relationships and which disappeared after one gift. Instead of guessing, they had data. They adjusted their thank you sequences, adjusted their ask amounts, and adjusted their messaging based on what their donors actually told them through their giving patterns.

Back to Fundraising

The person who used to spend half their week managing CSV files and reconciling data now runs campaigns and analyses what's working. Manual data work went from hours each week to nothing. Reports that used to take two days are available instantly.

Email segmentation now runs on actual donor behaviour instead of best guesses. They can see who their major donors are, who's lapsed, and who's about to churn. The system tells them. They respond accordingly, not weeks later when the next batch report arrives.

The team feels the difference in how they move. Campaigns launch faster. Decisions get made faster. Results are visible immediately. There's no more waiting for data.

The Freedom to Innovate

With real-time data and no manual overhead, the team is experimenting. They're testing different ask levels, different messaging, different timing. Every test produces data they can act on immediately. That velocity compounds. Six months in, they've learned more about their donor base than they did in years under the old system.

The thing about removing manual processes isn't just efficiency. It's psychological. When you're not drowning in data management, you have room to think about strategy. Orphans & Mead is now thinking about strategy.
