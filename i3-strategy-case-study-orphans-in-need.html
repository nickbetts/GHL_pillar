Orphans In Need: Growing donations 25% through connected operations

How a UK Muslim charity unified their website, donor data, and Islamic calendar campaigns to build sustainable year-round fundraising.

Fragmented Systems, Seasonal Chaos

Orphans In Need was operating with separate systems that didn't talk to each other. Donor records were scattered. Website campaigns lived in one place, CRM data in another, marketing schedules nowhere coordinated. During Islamic calendar peaks—Ramadan, Qurbani, Muharram—the team was manually coordinating across platforms, pulling reports from different systems, hoping nothing fell through the cracks.

The real pain point was operational. They knew who their donors were, they had good campaigns planned, but the infrastructure meant half their effort went into moving data around instead of connecting with people.

Two Website Rebuilds Without Losing a Donor

In three years, Orphans In Need went through two complete website iterations. Each rebuild was an opportunity to improve donor experience, streamline giving flows, and evolve their approach. The critical thing was that every rebuild preserved donor history and data. Each evolution built on what was already working instead of starting from scratch.

Their first website on i3MEDIA was solid. But after a year they knew they could do better. Campaign pages could be more compelling. Giving flows could be simpler. They rebuilt the entire site. Their donors didn't even notice because the transition was seamless. A year later, they refined it again based on what they'd learned.

That only works when the platform preserves your data through migrations. With disconnected systems, a rebuild meant risk. With i3MEDIA, a rebuild meant improvement without disruption.

More Donors Staying, Fewer Walking Away

Donations are up 25%. Donor retention is up 30%. And drop-off during giving is down 35%. For a charity dependent on recurring and repeat giving, those numbers compound. A donor who finishes their gift instead of dropping off becomes a donor who gives again next year. One good year of retention becomes three years of growth.

Islamic Calendar Campaigns That Actually Run On Schedule

Muslim giving follows the Islamic calendar, not the Gregorian one. Ramadan generosity is different from Qurbani giving, which is different from Muharram remembrance campaigns. Before i3MEDIA, coordinating campaigns across these peaks meant manual scheduling and hoping the right message got to the right donors at the right time.

Now campaigns are planned months in advance and execute reliably. Ramadan comes, the campaign runs. Qurbani comes, the thank you sequences go out automatically. Year-round giving strategy with peaks that actually happen when intended instead of scrambled last-minute execution.

Connected data means they know which donors respond to which campaigns. Ramadan campaign performance informs Qurbani strategy. Success patterns repeat because the data is visible. That kind of learning only works when systems talk to each other.
