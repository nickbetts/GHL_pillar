Thirteen Years, Multiple Platforms, One Constant

Mustafah came on board in 2013. We launched their first site and took on their digital marketing and SEO, which performed really well. Over the years we expanded globally, launching Mustafah USA, Canada and Pakistan on their legacy platform.

By 2024, the legacy system had become a real bottleneck. Pages took weeks to go live because each territory ran its own separate instance. Currency conversions were hardcoded. Donor data lived in three separate systems (the legacy platform, a standalone Salesforce instance, and spreadsheets). Reporting meant manual pulls and spreadsheet reconciliation across geographies. Ramadan campaigns meant late nights syncing donation pages and email sequences across four territories on different server infrastructure. Geographic expansion was basically blocked because the legacy platform wasn't built for scaling to new regions.

By early 2025, it was obvious they needed a unified platform. But the risk was very real. Mustafah processes thousands of donations daily across five territories with different currencies (GBP, USD, CAD, PKR, AUD) and local payment methods. A migration failure meant days of lost fundraising during their peak season. Not acceptable.

Five Countries, Zero Downtime

In 2025, Mustafah moved to Pillar. This was not a single site migration. Five live territories with their own data, currencies, payment methods, and donor lifecycles all moving simultaneously. We built a parallel infrastructure: Pillar ran alongside the legacy system for three weeks, both receiving donations.

The migration approach was careful and methodical. We pulled 18 months of historical donation data (156,000+ donor records) from the legacy platform and the spreadsheet systems, deduplicated everything against their Salesforce instance, and validated every single record. Email sequences, scheduled campaigns, and calendar-driven content got mapped and rebuilt. We configured Salesforce OAuth to replace their manual CRM syncs, so now updates flow bidirectionally in real time without anyone having to touch a spreadsheet.

Payment provider integrations were the trickiest bit. We staged each territory's checkout on Pillar and ran parallel transactions for a week to catch weird edge cases (manual gifts, corporate matches, recurring donation setup). On cutover day, we switched DNS and payment routing in a 14-minute window at 2 AM UTC, specifically timed to avoid peak giving hours anywhere. Zero downtime. Nothing broke. No lost records.

By mid-2025, Australia was live. Canada, US, and Pakistan migrated over the following eight weeks, each one reusing the validated playbook.

Ramadan 2026: 47% Uplift in Unique Donor Revenue

First Ramadan on Pillar: 47% lift in donation volume from new and lapsed donors. Here's why that actually happened: in the legacy system, campaign pages had to be requested 10 days in advance, built centrally, then staged in each territory. By Ramadan week 2, it was basically a choice between freezing the campaign or risking donor experience falling apart across geographies.

On Pillar, a fundraiser in any territory can spin up a new donation page in 20 minutes. No backend involvement. No 10-day lead time. During Ramadan week 3, when they needed a campaign for Ramadan Box distributions in Pakistan, the local team created it and tested it live in two hours. The Calendar Manager synced it into email sequences and SMS automatically. Recurring donation setup converted at 3.2x their legacy system baseline because the flow actually worked.

No scrambling. No manual patches. The team ran the campaign, not the platform.

Now They Run It

The inhouse team is trained up and operating Pillar independently. Fundraisers create pages directly without waiting on backend. Salesforce API sync replaced their manual exports. When a donor updates their payment method in Pillar, it updates Salesforce immediately and the CRM triggers nurture campaigns automatically. Territory managers have a unified reporting dashboard where they can see donation trends by region, campaign performance by giving level, and donor acquisition costs. All live. Not yesterday's spreadsheets.

Operationally, page creation time dropped from 10 days (request, build, test, deploy times five territories) to 20 minutes. Email list segmentation runs on real-time data instead of weekly CSV exports. Recurring donation setup, which was failing silently before, now converts at a real rate and they can see exactly where donors drop off. Campaign iteration changed from "plan months in advance" to "test Monday, scale Wednesday."

During Ramadan, they're not scrambling to patch things. They're fundraising.
