i3MEDIA GTM Playbook: LinkedIn Ads

Targeted paid social awareness for Muslim charity decision-makers and operations leads.

1st Touch | LinkedIn Ads (Highly Targeted ICP Brand Awareness)

B2B Paid Awareness

1. Targeting Demographics & Operators Filters

Isolate account access profiles by using strict boolean filtering to reach decision-makers in Muslim charities across the UK:

  • Targeted Company Industries: Non-profit Organisation Management, Philanthropy, Civic & Social Organisations.
  • Muslim Charity Account Size Tiers: Segmented into 1-200 employees and 11-500 employees.
  • Job Titles & Functional Seniority: Founder, Co-Founder, Chief Executive Officer, Managing Director, Owner, Operations Director, Operations Manager, Head of Fundraising, Fundraising Director, Digital Operations Lead, Marketing Manager, Head of Marketing, Director, VP, Manager, C-Level.

2. Creative Concept and Copy Hook Layouts

Operations Manager Setup (Lead Gen & Awareness)

Format: Single Image Creative

Visual Concept: Clear split-screen showing desktop tool fragmentation vs a unified i3MEDIA environment.

Copy Script: Stop fighting your technology stack. Stretched thin synchronising entries manually across legacy spreadsheets? Recover admin time and route every enquiry, campaign response, and supporter journey through one accountable system.
Headline Options: i3MEDIA: one growth system, no tool sprawl. / Run strategy, CRM, and campaigns from one stack.
CTA: Learn More.

Fundraising Director Setup (Thought Leadership)

Format: Document Lead Ad

Visual Concept: Dynamic multipage PDF layout overview previewing modern retention strategies.

Copy Script: Prove campaign ROI to leadership with clean attribution and repeatable reporting. Stop guessing where budget should go next. Discover how delivery teams use segmented automation and channel intelligence to improve retention and conversion quality.
Headline Options: Turn campaign activity into board-ready evidence. / The practical playbook for measurable growth.
CTA: Download.

3. Campaign Architecture and Budget Split (Best-Practice Baseline)

Campaign Layer Objective Audience Build Budget Guidance Primary Conversion Signal
Awareness Layer Reach and qualified traffic Core titles plus adjacent functions, segmented by Muslim charity size 40% of spend Qualified profile visits and page engagement depth
Education Layer Content consumption and problem framing Document ad and video engagers, plus site visitors with time-on-page thresholds 35% of spend High-intent content actions and repeat visits
Lead Capture Layer Lead form completions and qualified hand-raise intent Warm audiences only, with customer and booked-demo suppression 25% of spend MQL-quality lead submissions

4. Creative System by Buying Stage

Awareness Creative

  • Formats: single image plus short captioned video.
  • Message style: problem-first with one clear operational pain point per asset.
  • Frequency principle: rotate hooks fortnightly to avoid audience fatigue.

Education Creative

  • Formats: document ads and carousel explainers.
  • Message style: practical frameworks, benchmark snapshots, and role-specific proof points.
  • Destination principle: route to matching educational pages, not demo pages by default.

Lead Capture Creative

  • Formats: lead gen forms and high-trust single image variants.
  • Message style: implementation clarity, expected outcomes, and next-step confidence.
  • CTA principle: use one primary CTA per ad set to preserve signal quality.

5. Lead Form and CRM Handoff Standards

  • Field design: collect only essential routing fields at first touch: work email, role, organisation type, primary challenge.
  • Hidden fields: capture campaign, ad set, creative ID, and audience tier for pipeline attribution.
  • Handoff SLA: first human follow-up inside one business day for high-intent form fills.
  • Suppression rules: booked-demo and customer status must sync back into LinkedIn audience exclusions daily.

6. Measurement and Optimisation Cadence

Cadence Primary Review Focus Action Type
Twice weekly Spend pacing, delivery anomalies, audience saturation Budget rebalance within active campaigns
Weekly Lead quality by audience and creative message Pause weak variants, promote top-performing hooks
Fortnightly Creative fatigue and message overlap Inject net-new concepts and refresh underperforming angles
Monthly Pipeline contribution and conversion quality by segment Reallocate investment to highest-yield audience clusters
