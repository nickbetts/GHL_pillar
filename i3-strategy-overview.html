Brand Positioning

One-line position: i3MEDIA is the in-house digital growth partner that combines strategy, web, CRM, SEO, paid media, and social execution to deliver measurable outcomes without tool sprawl or handoff friction.

What that means in practice

i3MEDIA operates as one joined-up delivery team across brand, website, CRM, search, paid, and lifecycle channels. That means fewer delays between strategy and execution, cleaner data, and campaigns that can be scaled quickly once they prove impact.

We support organisations across charity, retail, healthcare, and education, with deep charity-sector expertise where donation flows, supporter journeys, and data confidence are mission-critical. We keep the same operating logic for every account: diagnose, build, launch, measure, and optimise.

Brand Voice

We sound like

  • Warm and human. We talk to Muslim charity teams like people, not procurement committees.
  • Practical and direct. No waffle. Real outcomes, plain language.
  • Slightly playful. We can raise an eyebrow at spreadsheet culture without being smug about it.
  • Quietly confident. We know what we do well. We do not need to shout it.
  • British. Organised, understated, and occasionally self-aware.

We do not sound like

  • Corporate jargon that sounds like a consultancy deck
  • Preachy or moralistic copy
  • Overly casual internet slang
  • Cold, technical wording with no human tone
  • Vague filler with no clear meaning

Tone by Channel

ChannelToneExample
Google Ads headlines Direct and outcome-focused. One clear idea per line. "One delivery partner from strategy to growth" not "Complex platform message"
Meta ad copy Conversational opener, specific pain, warm payoff. "If your team is still copying data between three tools on a Monday morning, this is for you."
LinkedIn company posts Useful and grounded. Teach something small, then let it land. "Most teams do not lose growth because they lack ideas. They lose it in handoff gaps between strategy, content, paid, and CRM."
Founder LinkedIn posts First-person, direct, occasionally dry. Real opinions, not corporate safe. "We had a call last week with a Muslim charity team of four running everything on a spreadsheet from 2017. The spreadsheet had a name."
Email subject lines Plain and curious. Reads like it came from a real person. "How many hours did your team spend on admin last week?" not "Your Q2 Operational Efficiency Resource Is Ready"
Landing page headlines Reassuring and clear. Tell them what it is and why it matters. "Your growth stack, delivered in one system" not "Generic infrastructure wording"

Things we always say

  • In-house and full-service. Strategy to execution sits in one delivery team
  • Measurable outcomes over vanity metrics: qualified pipeline, conversion quality, and retained value
  • Cross-sector capability with strong charity depth: charity, retail, healthcare, and education
  • Evidence-led decision making: attribution clarity, channel accountability, and weekly keep/kill reviews
  • Admin time recovery. Only claim this with a real customer number behind it

Category Positioning Route

Primary message: i3MEDIA is the in-house growth partner for ambitious organisations. Secondary proof route: i3MEDIA has deep charity-sector delivery experience, especially where donations and supporter journeys are central.

  • Category framing: full-service digital growth delivery under one roof
  • Proof framing: 20+ years, in-house delivery model, and a visible portfolio across sectors
  • Execution rule: any superlative claim must have a dated source note and evidence table in the Trust Sprint page
