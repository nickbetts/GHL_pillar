Brand Positioning

One-line position: Pillar is the all-in-one platform that gives Muslim charities in the UK a proper website, a CRM that actually works, and donation tools to grow, without tool sprawl, spreadsheet admin, or six-figure enterprise pricing.

What that means in practice

We are built by people who spent 15 years helping Muslim charities handle cobbled-together systems and manual workarounds. We made Pillar because Muslim charity teams in the UK deserve the same quality of digital infrastructure that bigger organisations take for granted, without needing a full tech department to run it.

Our customers are operations managers who have had enough of spreadsheets, fundraising directors who need cleaner data for trustees, and digital leads who want one platform they can hand to the whole team. We help Muslim charity teams move from firefighting to sustainable growth.

Brand Voice

We sound like

  • Warm and human. We talk to Muslim charity teams like people, not procurement committees.
  • Practical and direct. No waffle. Real outcomes, plain language.
  • Slightly playful. We can raise an eyebrow at spreadsheet culture without being smug about it.
  • Quietly confident. We know what we do well. We do not need to shout it.
  • British. Organised, understated, and occasionally self-aware.

We do not sound like

  • Corporate jargon that sounds like a consultancy deck
  • Preachy or moralistic copy
  • Overly casual internet slang
  • Cold, technical wording with no human tone
  • Vague filler with no clear meaning

Tone by Channel

ChannelToneExample
Google Ads headlines Direct and outcome-focused. One clear idea per line. "One CRM built for Muslim charities" not "Complex platform message"
Meta ad copy Conversational opener, specific pain, warm payoff. "If your team is still copying data between three tools on a Monday morning, this is for you."
LinkedIn company posts Useful and grounded. Teach something small, then let it land. "Most Muslim charities do not lose donors because of bad fundraising. They lose them because the follow-up got buried."
Founder LinkedIn posts First-person, direct, occasionally dry. Real opinions, not corporate safe. "We had a call last week with a Muslim charity team of four running everything on a spreadsheet from 2017. The spreadsheet had a name."
Email subject lines Plain and curious. Reads like it came from a real person. "How many hours did your team spend on admin last week?" not "Your Q2 Operational Efficiency Resource Is Ready"
Landing page headlines Reassuring and clear. Tell them what it is and why it matters. "Your whole Muslim charity operation, in one place" not "Generic infrastructure wording"

Things we always say

  • "Muslim charities in the UK" specifically, not "nonprofits" as a default (use "nonprofits" only where US or international context applies)
  • Fundraising and CRM in one. This is the core product truth, so lead with it often
  • No platform transaction fees. This is a real differentiator, so say it plainly
  • Built on 15 years of sector experience. Say it once per channel, not on every page
  • Admin time recovery. Only claim this with a real customer number behind it

Muslim Charity Positioning Route

We are going to prioritise a Muslim charity category position in paid search, landing pages, and trust content.

  • Category framing: fastest growing Muslim charity CRM
  • Proof framing: most years of Muslim charity sector experience and most Muslim charities served
  • Execution rule: any "fastest growing" or "most" claim must only go live with a dated source note and evidence table in the Trust Sprint page
